Introduction

More about me and my interest in ethical and safe AI

20 Apr 2026 · 4 min read

Ever since high school I had two main areas of interest. Technology and Psychology. In my final year, I conducted an independent research project focusing on the long-term effects that Instagram can have on high-schoolers in Australia. This project was the first major intersection in which I was able to merge my two passions together in a meaningful way, and was a great first experience to introduce me to the world of Ethics in AI.

During my studies as a software engineer, I have been fortunate enough to develop a technical based framework, mastering coding fundamentals and understanding how systems are built from the ground up. This knowledge has helped reinforce my beliefs that the ethical use of AI and safety regulations regarding it should be seen as the number one priority during implementation, rather than as simply an afterthought at the end of a development cycle.

What I'm trying to do

I'm hoping to delve into what specific guidelines should be implemented into governance, focusing on pre-existing examples of successful implementations. As this area is still so new and rapidly changing, I'm also hoping to develop my own general set of guidelines that can be used in my own practice when building AI systems. This might look like a list of things to check before deploying an already existing AI model into a new system, or a more detailed set of rules that can be followed when building one's own AI system to ensure user safety is prioritised.

Aside from that though, I am mainly hoping to just write about what interests me the most. Some areas I am excited to delve into include: How can LLM's be safely integrated into company usage, To what extent does the use of AI integrated systems reduce the number of human jobs, and What is the defining point of AI consciousness
